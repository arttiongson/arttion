I ran $65 million in store operations before I ever wrote a line of code. That's why I build differently.

Before moving into product accounting, I led store operations at a major national retailer — managing a $65M book of business, leading cross-functional teams, redesigning operational workflows, training staff on new systems, and owning the P&L outcomes.

I know what operational friction looks like because I've spent years fighting it from the inside.

When I moved into product accounting, I found myself working inside Microsoft Dynamics NAV, running monthly close processes and managing inventory reconciliation. Within my first month, I looked at a reconciliation workflow that consumed 2-3 days every cycle and I decided to fix it.

Using AI as my development partner, I built an automated Power Query pipeline pulling live data through ODBC connections directly from our ERP database. What took days now takes one click.

That first win set the trajectory. Using AI, I went on to build an internal inventory portal in ASP.NET Core with C# and Razor Pages, Ombri — a personal finance iOS app, Music Tool — an iPadOS reference app for guitar, ukulele, and bass, a Python-powered Discord bot with slash commands and music queuing, and this portfolio site you're looking at right now.

I don't position myself as a developer. I'm an operations-minded builder who uses AI to ship at the speed of a full engineering team. I see where processes break down, and I have the tools and the fluency to fix them.
